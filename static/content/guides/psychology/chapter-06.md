### 6.1 Matching Methods to Developmental Stages

Each stage of the Spiral has its own logic, rhythm, and needs. What feels supportive at one stage may feel confusing or threatening at another.

### 6.3 Therapeutic Pitfalls and Misses by Stage

We all carry implicit assumptions about what growth looks like. But if we‚Äôre not careful, we may mistake our own next step for the client‚Äôs current truth.

This section highlights common pitfalls ‚Äî and how to stay grounded in attuned presence.

#### Common Pitfalls

| Pitfall                         | What It Looks Like                           | How to Reattune                             |
|----------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| **Stage projection**             | ‚ÄúYou‚Äôre clearly ready to move into Yellow‚Ä¶‚Äù   | Ask: ‚ÄúWhat *they* are ready for ‚Äî not me?‚Äù   |
| **Idealization of later stages**| ‚ÄúTurquoise is where real healing happens.‚Äù     | Honor every stage as sacred and sufficient   |

| **Acceleration pressure**       | ‚ÄúYou‚Äôre awakening! Let‚Äôs move through this!‚Äù   | Respect pacing, consent, nervous system      |
| **Flattening complexity**       | ‚ÄúEveryone‚Äôs just in a process, nothing matters.‚Äù| Don‚Äôt erase suffering with abstraction       |

| Therapist Stage | Common Bias                            | Missed Opportunity                         |

- How might my favorite methods reflect my own stage center?

### 6.4 Modalities Across the Spiral

#### Modality Emphasis by Stage

| Stage     | Somatic Focus                            | Cognitive Focus                          | Narrative Focus                              |
|-----------|-------------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------|

| **Purple**| Ritual movement, rhythmic grounding       | Magical causality, symbolism              | Mythic stories, ancestor talk, group lore     |

| **Yellow**| Pattern tracking, nervous system literacy | Systems thinking, meta-reflection         | Reframing life as nested systems or seasons   |

#### How to Use This Mapping

1. Notice the client‚Äôs preferred channel ‚Äî do they gesture, argue, emote, describe dreams?
2. Match your entry point to their stage ‚Äî not yours.
3. Expand gently outward ‚Äî once safety and resonance are established, try adding an adjacent mode.

Example:
- A Red stage client may begin with somatic assertion (punching pillows, movement therapy)
- But over time, they may benefit from narrative hero reframing or even simple cognitive goal-setting

#### Modality Flexibility Across Stages

- Somatic work is universal ‚Äî but the *language around the body* must shift: safety (Beige), energy (Red), harmony (Green), intelligence (Yellow).
- Cognitive work needs calibration ‚Äî too much abstraction too soon can destabilize.
- Narrative work is deeply adaptive ‚Äî but always check whose voice is being internalized.
